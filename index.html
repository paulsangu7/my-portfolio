About Me

Paulo Kiwalile is a Tanzanian regional development planner and digital innovation enthusiast with a passion for harnessing technology to support communities. With a solid academic foundation and practical experience in geospatial mapping, AI, and data tools, Paulo blends planning expertise with digital skills to deliver impactful solutions.

He holds a Bachelor of Science in Regional Development Planning from Institute of Rural Development Planning and has pursued specialized training in digital agriculture, artificial intelligence, data visualization, and community mapping. Paulo has worked with organizations such as the Humanitarian OpenStreetMap Team (HOT), FAO, and AI4D Africa, contributing to open data initiatives and capacity building in emerging technologies.

Through various projects and short courses, he continues to explore the intersection of technology and development, aiming to promote data-driven decision-making, inclusion, and sustainable growth.
